# 🎓 india-engineering-education-analysis

The **Indian Engineering Education Analysis** is a web scraping and data analytics project that collects, cleans, and analyzes engineering college data across India. The project evaluates accessibility, quality, placement outcomes, accreditation, return on investment (ROI), and regional disparities in engineering education.

The objective is to transform publicly available college information into actionable insights for students, parents, educational institutions, and policymakers.

---

## 🎯 Problem Statement

Choosing the right engineering college is challenging due to scattered and inconsistent information across multiple sources.

The objective of this project is to:

- Collect engineering college data through web scraping.
- Clean and standardize the extracted data.
- Analyze educational quality and placement outcomes.
- Identify regional gaps in engineering education.
- Generate insights that support informed decision-making.

## 📊 Key Insights

- Engineering colleges are concentrated in a few major states, while several regions remain underserved.
- Private colleges significantly outnumber government institutions.
- Placement performance varies widely across states and institutions.
- Higher tuition fees do not always translate into better placement outcomes.
- Colleges with stronger accreditation generally show better placement performance.
- ROI differs substantially among institutions, highlighting the importance of evaluating both cost and career outcomes.

---

## 💼 Business Recommendations

- Increase investment in engineering education in underserved regions.
- Encourage institutions to improve placement support and industry collaborations.
- Strengthen accreditation and quality assurance initiatives.
- Improve transparency in placement and fee reporting.
- Focus on improving ROI through better industry engagement and skill development.
